Access Code Program Director

September 2014 - June 2016

I worked with the team at Coalition for Queens (C4Q) to build and launch the Access Code program: a 9-month intensive developer training program for adults from diverse and low-income backgrounds. I joined the C4Q team in 2014 as the third full-time team member (in addition to the two co-founders), and saw the team grow to 18 over my two year tenure, directly managing a team of 4.

My primary role with the team was to lead the design and execution of Access Code, building upon the successes of the pilot program. In my time with the team, we ran two cohorts (one iOS and one Android) and built the curricula from scratch, recruited and engaged a community of 100+ technical volunteers, and engaged community partners to receive 1200+ applications for the 62 student slots. Graduates from this program have been placed at top-tier companies like Jet, OKCupid, Capital One, Pinterest, Kickstarter, and more, and the average income has increased from $18,000 pre-program to $85,000 3-months post-program.

Outside of this role, I wore many hats at C4Q as we grew the team to support our programs. I participated in various initiatives spanning everything from grant writing and partnership development to organizational growth planning and the development of ops systems.
